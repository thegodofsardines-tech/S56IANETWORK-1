I. The Genesis: Out of the Shadows of ISD

Before the Intelligence Agency (IA) became the Foundation’s premier global eye, intelligence was fractured. The Internal Security Department (ISD) watched the inside; external threats were handled by isolated site-specific cells.

Enter Mr. Ashe T. As a high-ranking ISD Counter-Intelligence Advisor, Ashe T spent decades hunting moles, predicting Chaos Insurgency infiltrations, and mastering the art of the preemptive strike. He realized before anyone else that the Foundation was fighting a blind war. The anomalous world was expanding, and ISD's inward focus left the Foundation vulnerable from the outside. Thanks to his enormous clout and flawless reputation, Ashe T drew up the plan for a unified and preemptive espionage division: the Intelligence Agency. Seeing how promising this was and aware of the need for such an organization at the Foundation, O5-3 intervened, giving the Council Approval. Ashe T was promoted to Agency Director, establishing a strict, streamlined hierarchy: Clearance Level 5: Director (Ashe T) & Assistant Director Clearance Level 4: The Directorative Board Clearance Level 3: Field Agents and Operatives Though born in the shadows of Sector 1, the newly formed IA needed a central processing hub—a place secure enough to hold the world's most dangerous secrets, far from the prying eyes of rival Groups of Interest. They chose an isolated, deep-sea titan in the Arctic Ocean.

II. Site-56 and the Nightmare of Sector Theta

Located far off the icy coast of Novaya Zemlya, Site-56 was never meant to be an intelligence hub.

Masked from the world by a massive, weathered oil rig plunging into the freezing Arctic waters, the actual facility is a subterranean behemoth bored deep into the oceanic bedrock. Officially, Site-56 was a maximum-security containment installation designed for world-ending threats. Its deepest sub-level, Sector Theta (S4/S5), was the most secure sector in the entire Foundation network, fortified by an intricate matrix of localized miniature Reality Anchors. Director Ashe T anchored the IA’s main operations command to the upper levels of Site-56. It was a perfect marriage of convenience: the Agency got the ultimate fortress, and the site got a hyper-vigilant counter-intelligence grid. Then came the day the anchors failed. [WARNING: DATA CORRUPTION DETECTED] [RECOVERED LOG: TIMELINE X-0] [LOCATION: SITE-56, SECTOR THETA] An unexpected, catastrophic power outage knocked the mini-Reality Anchors offline. Without giving the Site any preparation, SCP-001 ("The Unseelie Queen") initiated a breach. Her mere presence began unraveling the physical laws of the facility. Interestingly enough, [ >The Administrator< ] whose real identity is Top Secret with Level 6 Clearance, was present at Site-56 conducting a secret inspection. With containment broken and Unseelie Queen just seconds away from breaking out into the outside world, The Administrator took the final executive order. They activated "The Kraken Protocol." A battery of silos hidden beneath the Arctic ice opened. A barrage of ICBMs and tactical nuclear warheads were redirected, designated to detonate directly on top of Site-56, vaporizing the facility, the oil rig, and everything within a ten-mile radius.

III. The Snapped Thread: The Loop

The nukes never hit. Or rather, they never finished hitting.

In an effort to protect herself, SCP-001 utilized an immense amount of mysterious power. With the missiles entering the earth’s atmosphere, the Unseelie Queen actually cut through the very fabric of reality itself. She didn't free herself; instead, she dragged the entire perimeter of Site-56—including the oil rig above and the IA headquarters inside—into a localized, inescapable timeloop. Properties of the Site-56 Temporal Anomalous Zone: Duration: The loop lasts between 3 to 7 days, always resetting to the exact beginning of the week. Retention: Unlike standard temporal anomalies, all memories are perfectly retained across resets. Everyone inside remembers every single loop. The Perimeter: The loop is strictly bound to the geographic perimeter of Site-56 and its surface rig. For the IA agents stationed there, reality became a permanent, recurring nightmare. Every week ends with the sky burning as the Kraken Protocol’s nukes descend, followed by a flash of light, and a sudden awakening back at Monday morning.

IV. The Outsider Dilemma & The Hoard

The IA is a global agency; it cannot stop functioning just because its main headquarters is trapped in a glitching reality.

Over time, Director Ashe T and his Directorative Board adapted to their chronological prison, turning the anomaly into a terrifyingly effective logistical advantage. Because the loop is localized, outsiders can enter the perimeter. Foundation supply lines, external IA agents, and couriers can cross into the Arctic zone unaffected by the temporal distortion. However, Site-56 operates under one absolute, unbreakable rule: CRITICAL DIRECTIVE: THE OUTSIDER RULE Any individual who was not caught in the initial manifestation of the time loop MUST exit the perimeter before the Kraken Protocol triggers at the end of the cycle. Failure to cross the perimeter boundary before the reset will bind the individual to the loop permanently, forcing them to suffer the weekly death and reset for eternity. This open-door anomaly birthed the IA’s most absurd asset: The Hoard. When items are brought into the perimeter by outsiders and left there through a reset, they become duplicated or permanently locked into the loop's baseline reality. Realizing this, Director Ashe T ordered a decades-long, grueling optimization project. Agents attempted to fiddle with the broken Reality Anchors to snap the loop, but after hundreds of futile, agonizing resets, they abandoned the effort and focused on exploitation. Over thousands of loops, external IA agents have methodically carted hyper-advanced technology, anomalous weaponry, prototype surveillance gear, and centuries worth of global intelligence data into the perimeter. To an outsider, the IA at Site-56 has acquired centuries worth of bleeding-edge technological evolution overnight. The Agency's main site boasts tech that the rest of the Foundation won't develop for another hundred years—all meticulously accumulated, reset by reset, death by death.

V. Current Status of the Agency

Today, the Intelligence Agency operates as a ghost in the machine. Director Ashe T directs global operations from a command center that is technically burning in a nuclear fire and resetting every few days.

IA field agents across the globe (CL3) gather intelligence and send reports to the Arctic Ocean. The Directorative Board (CL4) processes this data with a chilling advantage: they have an entire week to analyze a threat, and if they fail, the loop resets, giving them a literal "do-over" with all their memories intact. O5-3 watches from a safe distance, utilizing the IA as the Foundation’s ultimate, undying oracle. They are trapped, they are heavily armed with the tech of tomorrow, and they are watching everything.

The wind from the coast of Novaya Zemlya was not simply blowing but howling. In the Arctic Ocean, the heavy, rusted iron legs of the deep water oil platform creaked under the pressure of moving ice. To any passing satellite, it was a failing commercial venture. In reality, it was a lid on a tomb.

Deep beneath the freezing black water, bored into the very bedrock of the earth, lay Site-56. And inside its upper tiers, far away from the horrors locked in the deep, the Intelligence Agency was at work. Ashe T, the director, was seated in his command room, swirling the contents of an amber-colored glass from which any traces of ice had disappeared. His tired eyes stared at the timer that counted down the final moments to the launch. 00:14:32. fourteen minutes until the world ended. Again. Ashe T remembered when he was just a man. He had been a ruthless Counter-Intelligence Advisor for the Internal Security Department, a man who hunted moles in the dark. When he convinced O5-3 that the Foundation needed an aggressive, outward-facing eye—the IA—he thought he was building a legacy. It was all sorted out: Level 3 consisted of his field agents, Level 4 was for his loyal board members known as the “Directorative,” and he and his assistant made up Level 5. He had chosen Site-56 as their central hub because it was an indestructible, secure fortress. He hadn’t accounted for Sector Theta. He hadn’t accounted for the Queen. He was startled by the tinkling sound. The massive blast doors to his office room opened and in walked a trembling Level 3 IA messenger dressed in an extreme cold parka. He was breathing heavily, holding a titanium briefcase close to his body. "Director, I have the confidential telemetry information from the Chaos Insurgency cells in Berlin that you asked for. It... it took three weeks to intercept." the messenger panted out, already feeling himself begin shiver in the cool air inside the room. Ashe T didn't look up from his countdown. "Put it on the desk, son. And then turn around and run." The courier blinked, confused. "Sir?" 'Look at the clock,' directed Ashe T. His tone was eerily emotionless, not panicked but authoritative in the extreme. He stopped for just a moment. Sighing, he continued, 'You are an outsider. You crossed the perimeter boundary three hours ago to deliver that case. You have exactly fourteen minutes to get back to the surface, board the heavy-lift chopper, and clear the ten-mile perimeter." The courier looked at the terminal. 00:12:01. "If you are inside this perimeter when the clock hits zero," Ashe T said, finally looking the young man in the eye, "you belong to the Queen. You will feel your skin melt. You will feel the shockwave shatter your bones. And then you will wake up on Monday morning, right back at the helipad, remembering every single scream. Forever. Move." His expression became pale in almost the space of a blink of an eye. His briefcase was dropped to the iron desk; he whirled around and raced towards the lift. Ashe T sighed, pulling the briefcase toward him. He popped the latches. Inside lay a prototype quantum-encryption drive—technology the Foundation’s surface labs wouldn't officially invent for another thirty years. It was part of The Hoard. For hundreds of loops, DSI agents had been exploiting the anomaly. Outsiders brought bleeding-edge tech into the perimeter; the loop reset, but the items remained, duplicated and locked into the facility's baseline reality. The IA at Site-56 possessed an arsenal from the next century, paid for in the currency of an endless weekly execution. He remembered the first time it happened. The sudden, catastrophic power failure. The mini-Reality Anchors in Sector Theta snapping like twigs. SCP-001—The Unseelie Queen—breaking free, her reality-warping aura turning the lower levels into a surreal nightmare of flesh and glass. The Administrator had been on-site that day. A Level 6 ghost. When containment failed entirely, the Administrator didn't hesitate. They activated the Kraken Protocol. Ashe T shut his eyes, visualizing what he had seen on the screens: a dozen intercontinental ballistic missiles loaded with tactical nuclear warheads blasting off from their secret launch sites under the Arctic ice, turning, and coming straight back down on themselves. With her desperate surge of mysterious energy, she cut a sword into the fabric of space-time to turn aside the explosion. She didn’t get away; she just messed up the cosmic machinery. She trapped them in a loop. Three to seven days of agonizing tension, ending invariably in a nuclear flash, only to start the week over again. They had tried to fix it. God, they had tried. For the first fifty loops, Ashe T had his tech teams fiddle with the ruined Reality Anchors, hoping to stabilize the timeline. Every attempt was a futile, bloody failure. Eventually, they stopped trying to escape. They adapted. The terminal chimed again. A holographic projection flickered to life over his desk. The stern, shadowed silhouette of O5-3 appeared. "Director," the Overseer's voice echoed from a secure, outside line that bypassed the temporal anomaly. "Have you reviewed the Berlin data?" "Just receiving it now, Oversight," Ashe T replied calmly, sliding the drive into his terminal. "My analysts will have a full brief on the Insurgency's global movements ready for you." "You only have nine minutes before the reset, Ashe T." "Nine minutes is an eternity here, O5-3," Ashe T said, a grim, witty smile touching his lips. "We will analyze the data in the next loop. We’ll have a whole week to dissect it, predict their every move, and draft the counter-strike. By Monday morning your time, we will have a flawless strategy. We are the perfect oracle, aren't we? A bureau of ghosts." "The Council appreciates your sacrifice, Director." "We didn't choose it, Oversight. But we use it." The line cut out. T. Ashe rose from his seat and made his way towards the reinforced glass window at his office, gazing out at the huge underground grotto at Site-56. Below him, the members of the Level 4 Board were calmly filling out paperwork, unaffected by their imminent deaths. After all, they'd already been killed 300 times that year. Up there, high, high above the ice, the sky was starting to ominously glow red. The ice beneath the sky reflected the red glow of the sky. The Kraken Protocol was now about to enter its last phase. The warheads were breaking the atmosphere. Ashe T took a sip of his drink. The facility's klaxons began to wail, a deep, rhythmic thrumming that shook the floorboards. 00:00:03. 00:00:02. 00:00:01.
